Liability

Liability refers to the legal responsibility for one’s actions or omissions. In the context of legal agreements.
For example, if a company fails to deliver goods as promised in a contract, they may be held liable for any financial losses the other party incurs as a result.

Severability

Severability: A clause stating that if one part of the agreement is found to be invalid, the rest remains in effect.
For example, in a lease agreement, if a specific clause about pet restrictions is found to be invalid due to local laws, the severability clause ensures that the rest of the lease agreement remains valid and enforceable.
